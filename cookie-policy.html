1. Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how Floens Flooring Services ("we," "us," or "our") may use cookies and similar tracking technologies on this website (the "Service"). This policy should be read together with our Privacy Policy and Terms of Service.

By using our website, you consent to the use of cookies in accordance with this policy. If you do not agree to our use of cookies, you can adjust your browser settings or choose not to use this website.

2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used to help websites work efficiently, remember preferences, support forms, and provide information to website owners.

  • Session cookies: temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser.
  • Persistent cookies: cookies that remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them.
  • First-party cookies: cookies set by the website you are visiting.
  • Third-party cookies: cookies set by a domain other than the one you are visiting.

3. Types of Cookies We May Use

3.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They may support security, network management, accessibility, basic page operation, form handling, and cookie preference storage. You cannot opt out of cookies that are strictly necessary for the website to operate.

  • Cookie consent: remembers your cookie preferences where a consent tool is used.
  • Session management: supports stable browsing and basic website functionality.

3.2 Analytics Cookies

Analytics cookies help us understand how visitors interact with the website by collecting information such as page visits, traffic sources, device types, and general browsing behavior. If analytics tools are enabled, this information helps us improve website performance and user experience.

  • Google Analytics or similar tools: may track website traffic, page views, and user behavior in an aggregated or analytics-focused manner.
  • Performance monitoring: may help identify technical issues and optimization opportunities.

3.3 Functional Cookies

Functional cookies may enable enhanced website features, such as remembering selected preferences or temporarily supporting form use when you request flooring information, quotes, or service details.

  • Preference support: may remember selected website options where available.
  • Form support: may temporarily support quote request forms or service inquiry forms.

3.4 Marketing Cookies

Marketing cookies may be used to understand the effectiveness of advertising campaigns or to help show relevant ads on third-party platforms. These cookies may track visits across websites depending on the tools enabled.

  • Advertising networks: may help measure ad performance and engagement.
  • Social media platforms: may support sharing, campaign measurement, or platform-based tracking where such features are used.

4. Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party services that set cookies or similar technologies on your device. These may include analytics providers, advertising platforms, form tools, website hosting tools, or social media platforms. These third parties may collect information about your online activities across different websites according to their own policies.

We do not control third-party cookies. You should review the privacy and cookie policies of any third-party services you interact with.

5. How to Manage Cookies

You have the right to accept, reject, delete, or limit cookies. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can usually modify your browser settings to decline or delete cookies.

5.1 Browser Settings

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Common browsers such as Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Safari, and Microsoft Edge provide tools for blocking, deleting, or managing cookies.

5.2 Opt-Out Tools

You may also be able to opt out of certain third-party cookies through provider tools, including Google Analytics opt-out tools, the Digital Advertising Alliance, or the Network Advertising Initiative.

Note: blocking or deleting cookies may affect your experience on our website. Some features, including forms or preference tools, may not function properly if cookies are disabled.

6. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature. Because there is currently no uniform standard for how DNT signals should be interpreted, our website does not currently respond to DNT browser signals or similar mechanisms.
